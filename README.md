# Basic Ideas in Statistics

Statistics is about making sense of data: it helps us summarize information, spot patterns, and make decisions under uncertainty. At its core, statistics provides tools to describe data, draw conclusions about populations, and test whether observed effects are real or just due to chance.

---

## üìä Key Concepts in Statistics

### 1. Descriptive Statistics
- **Purpose:** Summarize and describe data.
- **Measures of Central Tendency:** Mean (average), Median (middle value), Mode (most frequent value).
- **Measures of Dispersion:** Range, Variance, Standard Deviation (how spread out data is).
- **Example:** Average test scores in a class.

### 2. Inferential Statistics
- **Purpose:** Make predictions or generalizations about a population based on a sample.
- **Tools:** Hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, regression analysis.
- **Example:** Estimating the average income of a city by surveying 500 residents.

### 3. Population vs. Sample
- **Population:** Entire group of interest (e.g., all voters in a country).
- **Sample:** Subset of the population used for analysis.
- **Key Idea:** Good sampling methods ensure results represent the population.

### 4. Variables & Data Types
- **Qualitative (Categorical):** Colors, names, labels.
- **Quantitative (Numerical):** Heights, weights, test scores.
- **Discrete vs. Continuous:** Whole numbers (number of children) vs. measurable values (height).

### 5. Probability
- **Definition:** The likelihood of an event occurring.
- **Example:** Probability of rolling a 6 on a fair die = `1/6`.

### 6. Distributions
- **Normal Distribution:** Bell-shaped curve, common in natural and social phenomena.
- **Uniform Distribution:** All outcomes equally likely.
- **Why Important:** Helps model uncertainty and variation.

### 7. Hypothesis Testing
- **Null Hypothesis (H‚ÇÄ):** Assumes no effect or difference.
- **Alternative Hypothesis (H‚ÇÅ):** Assumes there is an effect or difference.
- **P-value:** Probability of observing data as extreme as ours if H‚ÇÄ is true.
- **Example:** Testing if a new drug works better than a placebo.

### 8. Confidence Intervals
- **Definition:** Range of values likely to contain the true population parameter.
- **Example:** A 95% confidence interval for average height might be `[5.6, 5.9]` feet.

### 9. Correlation & Regression
- **Correlation:** Measures strength/direction of relationship between two variables.
- **Regression:** Models how one variable predicts another.
- **Example:** Relationship between study hours and exam scores.

### 10. Central Limit Theorem
- **Idea:** With large samples, the distribution of sample means tends to be normal, regardless of population distribution.
- **Importance:** Justifies many statistical methods.
